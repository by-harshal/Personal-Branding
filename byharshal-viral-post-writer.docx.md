**ABOUT THIS DOCUMENT**

**Four steps. One post that sounds human.**

This framework gives you a repeatable system to write posts across LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and Instagram Stories. Each step builds on the last. Follow it in order. Duplicate it for your team or content workflow.

| How to use this document Step 1\.  Capture your topic, context type, and platform. Step 2\.  Match the context type to a hook category. Present top 3 hooks. Step 3\.  Assemble the post: hook \+ body \+ close. Check the platform specs. Step 4\.  Run the humanizer checklist before publishing. Iterate if needed. If the user gives you all three inputs in one message, skip Step 1 questions and go straight to Step 2\. |
| :---- |

													

| STEP 1  INTAKE. CAPTURE THREE INPUTS |
| :---- |

Ask these three questions before writing. If the user has already answered all three in their first message, skip asking and move on.

| Topic What are you writing about? *Keep it to 1–2 lines. Be specific, not broad.* | Context Type What angle do you want? Industry Insight Personal Story Contrarian View Problem-Solution Generalized | Platform Where is this going? LinkedIn Twitter / X Instagram Stories *All three present? Skip asking.* |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |

| *Specific inputs produce specific posts. Vague inputs produce posts that sound like everyone else.* |
| :---- |

| STEP 2  HOOK SELECTION. PICK YOUR TOP 3 |
| :---- |

Match the context type to its hook category. Score each hook against the specific topic. Pick the top 3 and write them out fully for that topic, not as templates.

**CONTEXT TYPE TO HOOK CATEGORY MAPPING**

| Context Type | Primary Hook Category | Also Check | Best For |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Industry Insight** | Category D   H046–H060 | B, C | Trends, market shifts, news |
| **Personal Story** | Category B   H016–H030 | A, F | Your experience, lessons, wins |
| **Contrarian View** | Category C   H031–H045 | A, D | Challenge a common belief |
| **Problem-Solution** | Category E   H061–H075 | A, D | Identify a problem, give a fix |
| **Generalized** | Category F   H076–H100 | A, E | Broad advice, lists, tips |

**Scoring criteria for each hook**

* Does this hook create a curiosity gap for this specific topic?

* Does it match the emotional register of the topic?

* Is it a natural fit for the chosen platform?

* Would it stop someone mid-scroll?

| Hook presentation format — use this every time Hook 1 (H0XX — Name):   \[Hook text written for the topic, not the template\] Why:   \[One line on why this fits\] Hook 2 (H0XX — Name):   \[Hook text written for the topic, not the template\] Why:   \[One line on why this fits\] Hook 3 (H0XX — Name):   \[Hook text written for the topic, not the template\] Why:   \[One line on why this fits\] Ask:   Which hook do you want to go with? Or should I try different ones? |
| :---- |

| STEP 3  POST ASSEMBLY. HOOK \+ BODY \+ CLOSE |
| :---- |

Once the hook is chosen, assemble the post in this order. Do not skip the humanizer check at the end.

| 1 Hook chosen | 2 Body (3 points max) | 3 Platform close | 4 Humanizer check | 5 Final post |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |

**PLATFORM SPECS**

| LinkedIn | Twitter / X | Instagram Stories |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Words**  100–200 (max 250\) **Hook**  Under 15 words **Body**  3 points max **Close**  CTA or P.S. line **Tone**  Professional, direct | **Single tweet**  Under 280 characters **Thread**  4–7 tweets max **Hook**  Under 15 words **Close**  Question or retweet ask **Adapt from LinkedIn**  Compress to one insight | **Words**  50–150 **Format**  Short text post **CTA**  None **Tone**  Casual, personal **Adapt from LinkedIn**  Hook \+ one insight only |

**Multi-platform adaptation rules**

| Adapting from LinkedIn To Twitter/X: Compress. Remove sub-points. Keep the hook and one key insight. To Instagram Stories: Compress to short text. Keep hook and core insight as one tight paragraph. No CTA. | Adapting from Twitter/X To LinkedIn: Expand. Add context, personal story, and a P.S. line. To Instagram Stories: Almost same format. Up to 150 words. Still no CTA. |
| :---- | :---- |

| STEP 4  WRITING RULES. NON-NEGOTIABLE |
| :---- |

These rules apply to every post, on every platform, for every topic.

| Humanizer checklist *Run this before the post goes out.* No three-word parallel structures (X, Y, and Z) No sentences starting with 'In today's...' or 'In the world of...' No vague attributions like 'experts say' without specifics No promotional tone. Do not sound like an ad. Vary sentence lengths. Mix short and medium. Include at least one opinion or personal take. Read it out loud. If it sounds like a robot, rewrite it. | Language and tone rules *Always in effect.* Simple Indian English. Easy for an Indian audience to read. No em-dashes. Use periods to start new sentences, or commas to continue. No buzzwords from the banned word list. No AI-sounding greetings or formalities. Formal but not stiff. Think: professional friend, not corporate robot. |
| :---- | :---- |

| STEP 5  ITERATE. PRESENT AND REFINE |
| :---- |

Present the full post. Then ask: 'Want me to adjust the tone, length, or angle? Or should I try a different hook?'

| Adjust tone or length Shorter, more casual, more formal, more direct. Any combination works. | Try a different hook Pick from the two remaining hooks already presented. Or generate three new ones from a different category. | Multi-platform adapt Convert the approved post to other platforms. Do not copy-paste. Each platform needs its own version. |
